INFO 340 Project Proposal

Problem & Data Domain

While advances in healthcare technology have increased the quality of care for neurological diseases, there is still a health literacy gap between clinicians, caregivers, and patients. Because caregivers often lack a fundamental understanding of neurological disorders, they are unable to provide the most effective level of care. Similarly, patients may not be aware of what symptoms to track, disease progression rates, or risk factors that can be easily managed. As such, the main question we aim to answer is: how can we increase caregiver and patient health literacy to increase the effectiveness of at-home care for neurological diseases. Neurological diseases have been affecting over one-third of our population for years, causing major concerns like disabilities, deaths, and other health conditions. Patients who are affected by these diseases, along with caregivers and medical students, often struggle with accessing clear, educational information about the conditions of neurological diseases. This problem revolves around people having difficulty finding specific content related to their interests or researching different disorders in one database, which often causes them to lose motivation when organized information is not available online. There are many existing attempts to solve this problem, including websites like the “National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke”, which provide information about neurological diseases. However, they are known to be very text-heavy and only have a few interactive features. There are also other websites like Quizlet that let users study flashcards on these topics, but these tools do not provide a structured, gamified platform for users. We realized that most tools online do not include structured information about these diseases alongside gamified incentives, and we hope to achieve these features so that people who are affected by neurological diseases or want to learn more about them have the right platform to do so.

App Description

The application will be a centralized hub of data related to nervous system disorders that users can access for a variety of information. This data will be presented in the form of modules, similar to those that are used to train workers prior to a job. Users will be able to interact with any module at any time and learn about a variety of information regarding neurological diseases, including history, types, population statistics, early detection, and how to care for those living with these disorders. We also plan to gamify our project through these modules. At the end of each one, there will be a knowledge check, which can come in the form of a multiple choice quiz or matching puzzle. Users earn points based on their quiz scores, and their point totals are displayed in a leaderboard with their friends. Another main feature is an interactive pet shop that is based on user progress. Users earn points by completing modules and knowledge checks, which can be used in the pet shop to unlock and customize pet characters. The pet evolves as users progress through more modules, reflecting their learning and engagement within the app. Each of the modules in the app will cover a specific neurological disease. The contents of each module will include explanations of the condition, symptoms, risk factors, and progression. Modules will also cover different types of treatment, history, and population statistics. To support caregivers, each of the modules will provide guidance for daily care, safety considerations, and recognizing when medical attention is needed. Additional features such as a glossary of medical terms will also be included. The knowledge checks at the end will ask users questions related to the module’s content they just learned. Our app offers an accessible, user-friendly way to learn about neurological diseases that isn’t hidden inside dense text and littered with medical jargon. The modules and overall format of the app will be organized in an intuitive way to allow for easy navigation, and the search engine helps users to quickly find information on any condition they need. By using interactive, gamified activities, the app will promote user engagement and improve knowledge retention. The primary users of this app include patients with neurological conditions, caregivers, and students or anyone wanting to learn more about neurological diseases.
